# AI Product Management Curriculum

A self-paced curriculum for product managers working on AI products. Three weeks. No coding required.

---

## What This Covers

**Week 1 — Foundations**
How large language models work: tokens, training, inference, context windows, and hallucination. A survey of the current model landscape (Claude, GPT-4o, Gemini, Llama) with guidance on how to choose between them.

**Week 2 — Architecture**
How to augment LLMs with external data (RAG), build multi-step workflows (Agents), and decide between fine-tuning, prompting, RAG, and reasoning models. Includes structured outputs and prompt caching.

**Week 3 — Product**
Feasibility assessment, ROI calculation, success metrics, risk management, and writing a PRD for an AI feature.

## How to Use It

**If you have three weeks:** Read the files in order — Syllabus, Week 2, Week 3. Use Resources alongside each week. Complete the deliverable at the end of each week (Capability Matrix, Architecture Decision Tree, PRD).

**If you need a quick answer:** Open [Quick Reference](AI_PM_Quick_Reference.md). It has model selection flowcharts, an architecture decision matrix, a pre-launch risk checklist, and answers to common engineering questions.

**If you're short on time:** Read the [Quick Reference](AI_PM_Quick_Reference.md) first, then Unit 3 (model landscape) and Unit 8 (metrics and risk) for the highest-value coverage in under four hours.

---

## Scope

This curriculum covers text-based LLMs and the product decisions around them: model selection, architecture choices, evaluation, and risk. It does not cover deep learning theory, model training, MLOps, computer vision, or multimodal AI.
